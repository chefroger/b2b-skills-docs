# LinkedIn Article Post Patterns

## Observed Format That Works (June 2026)

### Structure
1. **Title**: Provocative insight or comparison (not product pitch)
2. **Intro**: Relatable buyer scenario + natural credibility statement
3. **Section dividers**: Em-dash (—) for visual breathing room
4. **Problem deep-dive**: Why the old way fails, cost to buyer
5. **Numbered benefits**: 3 reasons tied to buyer's daily work
6. **Procurement translation**: Technical advantages → buying decisions
7. **Value-led CTA**: Offer comparison sheet/test reports/samples

### Tone
- Conversational expert, not salesperson
- Opens with "If you've been working in..." or "Here's something I've noticed..."
- Uses "you" and "your" frequently
- Specific market references (Bangladesh, UAE, Sri Lanka)
- No pricing discussions in article body

### Hashtag Mix
- 2 broad: #PowerGrid #SmartGrid
- 2 niche: #PreformedHardware #ElectricalContracting
- 1 trend: #RenewableEnergy

### Length
- 800–1500 words
- 4–6 sections with dividers
- Each section 2–4 paragraphs
